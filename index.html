Electromagnetic waves

An EM wave is a self-propagating disturbance of electric and magnetic fields. The two fields oscillate in phase, at right angles to each other and to the direction the wave travels.

Accelerating charges

Whenever a charge accelerates — such as an electron driven back and forth by an alternating current — it radiates a changing electric field. That changing E-field induces a changing magnetic field, and the two sustain each other outward as a wave.

Propagation in vacuum

With no medium required, EM waves cross empty space at the speed of light, c ≈ 3×10⁸ m/s. This is what lets sunlight reach Earth and radio signals reach spacecraft.

Reflection, transmission & absorption

When a wave meets a material, its energy splits three ways: some reflects off the surface, some transmits through, and some is absorbed and converted to heat or re-radiated energy. The proportions depend on the material's properties.
